## What is LIME?

LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) is a tool used to explain how an AI model makes decisions.

It does not create a new model.  
Instead, it answers the question:

"Why did the AI predict spam or not spam?"

---

## How does it work?

When the AI predicts a message:

- LIME looks at the words in the text  
- It removes or perturbs parts of the text  
- It observes how the prediction changes  
- It identifies which words have the most impact  

---

## Example Output

('free', 0.8)  
('win', 0.6)  
('wife', -0.02)

### Interpretation:
- **Positive values (+)** → support the model’s prediction  
- **Negative values (−)** → oppose the prediction  
- **Larger magnitude** → more important  

---

## Bar Chart Visualization

The bar chart is used to visualize LIME results:

- Upward bars → support the prediction  
- Downward bars → oppose the prediction  
- Center line → 0 (neutral point)

---

## Summary

LIME is an AI explanation tool that helps us understand:

"Which words influenced the model’s decision?"

---

## Project Pipeline

Text → TF-IDF → Model → Prediction → LIME → Explanation → Bar Chart
